Sleep Health:

Based on the heatmap analysis of the dataset, I selected columns with an absolute correlation greater than 0.05 in relation to GPA. For occupations, I applied one-hot encoding, and although some occupations showed lower correlations, I included all of them to ensure a comprehensive analysis, even if it might increase model complexity. Additionally, I split the blood pressure into two columns (Systolic and Diastolic) but retained only Systolic due to the high correlation between the two. The final selected columns are: Gender, Age, Sleep Duration, Quality of Sleep, Physical Activity Level, Stress Level, BMI Category, Heart Rate, Daily Steps, Sleep Disorder, Systolic, and the one-hot encoded occupation columns: Doctor, Engineer, Lawyer, Manager, Nurse, Sales Representative, Salesperson, Scientist, Software Engineer, and Teacher.

Why is Encoding Important?

Machine learning algorithms work with numerical data. Categorical features like gender, region, or lifestyle need to be converted into numerical form to allow models to process and learn from these values. Without encoding:

The algorithm might not understand the relationship between categories.
It may interpret the categories as ordinal values (i.e., the algorithm may assume a ranking between categories) if not encoded properly.
